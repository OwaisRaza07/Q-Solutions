What is a Lithium Battery?

The term "lithium battery" refers to one or more lithium cells that are electrically connected. A cell is a single encased electrochemical unit, while a battery consists of multiple electrical"/ connected component cells A battery can also only be made up of a single cell, such as the common AA, C, or coin cell.

A lithium battery is like other types of battery. The lithium battery cells contain a positive electrode, a negative electrode, a separator, and an electrolyte solution Atoms or molecules with a net electric charge (ions) are transferred from a positive electrode to a negative electrode through an electrotyte solutiom Lithium cells store and release power by converting chemical potential energy into electrical energy using lithium ions or lithium metal. Electrotyte solutions allow ions to flow freely between the electrodes.

#3 Know the lithium battery classification

The regulations classify lithium batteries based on their size as 'Fully Regulated' or 'Excepted'.
